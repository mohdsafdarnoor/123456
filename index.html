‚úÖ Do's and ‚ùå Don'ts: Using AI for Job Applications

‚úÖ DO's

  • Use AI to draft ‚Äì Let ChatGPT create the first version, then personalize it heavily.
  • Customize for each job ‚Äì Never send the same AI-generated letter twice.
  • Fact-check everything ‚Äì AI can hallucinate company details or job requirements.
  • Add personal stories ‚Äì AI can't write about YOUR specific experiences.
  • Use prompts as templates ‚Äì Modify them with your actual background and skills.
  • Learn from AI suggestions ‚Äì Understand why certain phrases work in German applications.
  • Proofread manually ‚Äì Check grammar, especially German umlauts and formal language.

‚ùå DON'Ts

  • Don't copy-paste blindly ‚Äì Recruiters can spot generic AI text instantly.
  • Don't lie or exaggerate ‚Äì AI might suggest skills you don't have.
  • Don't skip research ‚Äì AI doesn't know the company culture; you must research it.
  • Don't use AI in interviews ‚Äì Real-time AI tools are detectable and unethical.
  • Don't ignore German norms ‚Äì AI trained on US data may suggest overly informal language.
  • Don't skip human review ‚Äì Always have a native speaker or career advisor check your final draft.
  • Don't over-rely on tools ‚Äì AI is a helper, not a replacement for your effort.

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Ethical Note: German employers value authenticity and honesty. Using AI to create your application materials is acceptable, but presenting AI-generated content as entirely your own work without personalization can be considered dishonest. Always add your personal touch and verify all information.
