CV Rewrite (No Experience)

Use this when: You have no work experience but want to highlight academic projects.

Act as a German recruiter. I am a Master's student in International Management applying for a Werkstudent position in [Field/Industry]. I have no direct work experience, but I have these academic skills: - [Skill 1] - [Skill 2] - [Project Name & Description] Please rewrite my CV summary and experience section to highlight my "trainability," "reliability," and "academic achievements." Use professional German business language (or English if specified).
Cover Letter (Short Email)

Use this when: Sending an application via email.

Draft a professional, concise email application (Anschreiben) for a Werkstudent role at [Company Name]. My details: - Name: [Your Name] - Degree: Master International Management - Key motivation: [Why you want this job] - Availability: 20 hours/week Tone: Formal, polite, and eager to learn. Keep it under 200 words.

Mandatory Internship (Pflichtpraktikum)
I am applying for a mandatory internship (Pflichtpraktikum) in Germany. My profile: - Degree: [Degree Name] - Specialization: [Specialization] - German Level: [A1/B1/C1] Please create a one-page, ATS-friendly CV structure. Translate my academic coursework [Course 1, Course 2] into practical skills that a German recruiter would value. Explicitly mention that this is a mandatory internship (exempt from minimum wage).

Point out any unclear elements for a German recruiter. 3. Suggest 3 concrete improvements to increase my chances next time.
