AI Prompts Library

Copy-paste prompts for ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini.

ðŸŽ“ Working Student (Werkstudent)

1. Orientation

Use this to find roles fitting your profile:

I am a Master's student in [Major] at a German university looking for a Werkstudent job. My profile: - Degree: [Degree Name] - Key Skills: [Skill 1, Skill 2] - Languages: [German Level], [English Level] - Availability: [Hours] per week Tasks: 1. Suggest 10 realistic Werkstudent job titles I should search for. 2. Explain which roles are typically open to international students with my language level. 3. List 5 companies in [City/Region] known for hiring student workers.
2. Application

Use this to rewrite your CV for academic strength:

Act as a German recruiter. I am applying for a Werkstudent position in [Field]. I have limited work experience, but I have these academic projects: - [Project 1 Name & Description] - [Project 2 Name & Description] Tasks: 1. Rewrite my CV summary to highlight my "academic strengths" and "transferable skills". 2. Position me as "reliable", "structured", and "eager to learn". 3. Draft a short, professional email (in German) to HR expressing interest in the role.
3. Interview

Use this to prepare for "No Experience" questions:

I have an interview for a [Role]. Please prepare strong answers to the question: "You don't have much practical experience. Why should we hire you?" Focus on: - My quick learning ability (provide an example based on [Skill]) - My theoretical foundation from university - My reliability and flexibility as a student
4. Onboarding

Use this to communicate professionally:

Draft a polite email to my manager (in German). Context: I need to adjust my working hours next week due to an exam at university. Tone: Professional, apologetic but clear, and offering a solution (making up hours later).

ðŸ’¼ Internship (Praktikum)

1. Orientation

Use this to identify Mandatory vs. Voluntary roles:

I need to find a Mandatory Internship (Pflichtpraktikum) for my degree in [Field]. Tasks: 1. Suggest 5-7 suitable roles aligned with my profile: [List Skills]. 2. Identify internships in [Industry] where German is not a strict requirement (English speaking). 3. Explain the key differences in application strategy for a Mandatory vs. Voluntary internship in Germany.
2. Application

Use this to optimize for ATS systems:

Here is my current CV: [Paste Text] Here is the job description for the internship: [Paste Text] Tasks: 1. Identify missing keywords relevant to this specific role. 2. Flag any unclear job titles or descriptions that a German recruiter might misunderstand. 3. Rewrite my "Education" section to better match the requirements. 4. Draft a German-style cover letter (Anschreiben) emphasizing my motivation.
3. Interview

Use this to practice cultural fit:

I have an interview for an internship at a [Startup/Corporate]. Tasks: 1. List 5 common German interview questions for internship candidates. 2. Help me answer questions about my "German language level" confidently without lying. 3. Conduct a mock interview with me: Ask one question, wait for my answer, then critique it.
4. Onboarding

Use this to clarify legal status:

I need to explain to my employer that this is a Mandatory Internship (Pflichtpraktikum). Draft an email attaching my university certificate. Key points: - The internship is required for my degree. - It lasts for [Number] months. - Confirming that I am exempt from social security contributions for this period.

ðŸ“– Master Thesis

1. Orientation

Use this to find company partners:

I want to write my Master's thesis with a company in [Industry]. My specialization is [Specialization]. Tasks: 1. Identify types of companies in Germany (Startups, SMEs, DAX) open to thesis collaborations in this field. 2. Propose 3 specific, business-relevant thesis topics that would solve real problems for companies in this sector. 3. List platforms or strategies to find these specific opportunities.
2. Application

Use this to pitch your topic:

Draft a cold email to a Head of Department at [Company] proposing a thesis collaboration. Topic Idea: [Topic] My Value: [Why this helps them] Tasks: 1. Frame the thesis as a "business solution", not an academic burden. 2. Address potential concerns about time commitment and supervision effort. 3. Keep it professional, concise, and persuasive (in German or English).
3. Interview

Use this to negotiate terms:

I have a meeting to discuss a potential thesis collaboration. Please help me prepare for these questions: 1. "How much time will our team need to spend supervising you?" 2. "What happens to the data/IP you generate?" (NDA concerns) 3. "How do you balance working for us vs. writing the thesis?" Provide strong, reassuring answers.
4. Onboarding

Use this to manage expectations:

I am starting my thesis at [Company]. Tasks: 1. Create a communication plan to keep my university supervisor and company supervisor aligned. 2. Draft a template for a "Weekly Progress Update" email to send to my company mentor. 3. Advise on how to handle situations where the company asks for operational work that distracts from the thesis.

ðŸš€ Full-Time Employment

1. Orientation

Use this to target the right roles:

I am graduating with a Master's in [Degree]. I have experience in [Experience]. Tasks: 1. Suggest 5 specific job titles I should target in Germany for a full-time entry-level role. 2. Identify transferable skills from my studies/internships relevant to these roles. 3. List the top 3 cities in Germany with the highest demand for this profile.
2. Application

Use this to professionalize your CV:

Rewrite my CV to position me as "Job-Ready" for a full-time [Role]. Current Weakness: [Describe weakness, e.g., only internships]. Tasks: 1. Rewrite bullet points to focus on "Impact" and "Results" (use numbers/metrics). 2. Optimize for German ATS systems (keywords: [List Keywords]). 3. Draft a cover letter explaining why I am looking to stay in Germany long-term.
3. Interview

Use this for behavioral prep:

Act as a hiring manager for a [Role]. Conduct a behavioral interview using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result). Ask me: "Tell me about a time you faced a conflict at work." Critique my answer for: Structure, Clarity, and German cultural fit.
4. Onboarding

Use this for your first 90 days:

I just started my first full-time job in Germany as a [Role]. Tasks: 1. Create a 30-60-90 day plan to impress my manager. 2. Explain the typical performance expectations for new hires in Germany. 3. Outline cultural "Do's and Don'ts" for building trust with German colleagues in the first month.
